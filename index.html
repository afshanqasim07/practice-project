Quaid-e-Azam, Muhammad Ali Jinnah

1876-1947

THE GREAT MAN OF PAKISTAN

Quaid-e-Azam, Muhammad Ali Jinnah, is revered as the founder of Pakistan. Born on December 25, 1876,
Jinnah was a prominent lawyer, politician, and leader of the All India Muslim League. He played a pivotal role
in advocating for the rights of Muslims in British India, leading to the creation of Pakistan in 1947. Jinnah's
vision for Pakistan was a nation where all citizens could live in harmony, free from discrimination. His
dedication to the cause of independence and his efforts in shaping the newly formed country have made him
a symbol of determination, leadership, and integrity in Pakistan's history.



Biographies

  • Founder of Pakistan and leader of the All India Muslim League.
  • Revered as the driving force behind the creation of Pakistan in 1947.
  • A visionary leader who championed the cause of a separate Muslim state in South Asia.
  • The principal architect of Pakistan's independence from British rule.
  • The title bestowed upon Jinnah, meaning "Great Leader" for his role in establishing Pakistan
